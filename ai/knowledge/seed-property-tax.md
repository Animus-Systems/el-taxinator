# Spanish property tax reference (2026)

> Last verified: 2026-04-01. Covers rental income rules, deductible expenses,
> local taxes on property ownership and sale, and wealth tax where relevant.
> Applies Canary Islands specifics where they differ from the state default.

## 1. Rental income — how it's taxed

Rental income is **rendimiento del capital inmobiliario** on Modelo 100,
feeding the **base general**. Two structural distinctions matter:

| Rental type | Tax treatment | 60% reduction? |
|-------------|---------------|----------------|
| Long-term residential (contract ≥ 1 year, tenant's habitual residence) | rendimiento del capital inmobiliario | **Yes** |
| Tourist / short-term (Airbnb-style, < 1 month contracts) | rendimiento del capital inmobiliario, **unless** you provide hotel-like services | No |
| Commercial (office, retail) | rendimiento del capital inmobiliario | No |
| With hotel-like services (reception, cleaning during stay, meals) | **rendimiento de actividad económica** — treat as autónomo activity | N/A |

### 1.1 The 60% reduction (long-term residential)

- Applied to **net positive** rendimiento (ingresos − gastos deducibles).
- Only on **properly declared** rental — AEAT disallows the reduction
  retroactively if the income is first declared during an inspection.
- Since **2024** the reduction has been reformed for new contracts:
  - **90%** in "zonas tensionadas" if rent is reduced ≥5% vs. the prior
    lease.
  - **70%** for new tenants aged 18–35 in zonas tensionadas.
  - **60%** baseline for all other long-term residential (legacy rate
    preserved for pre-2024 contracts).
- Negative rendimientos always fully deductible (no reduction).

### 1.2 Deductible rental expenses

All must be **ordinary and necessary** and paid during the year:

- **IBI** (Impuesto sobre Bienes Inmuebles) — full annual amount.
- **Community fees** (cuotas de comunidad).
- **Home insurance** on the rented property.
- **Mortgage interest** (NOT principal) — proportional to the % of the
  property that's rented.
- **Amortización del inmueble**: **3%** annual on the greater of
  (a) construction value in the catastral ficha, or
  (b) acquisition cost minus land value.
- **Amortización de muebles**: 10% of the furniture cost for furnished
  rentals.
- **Repair and conservation** (painting, plumbing, fixing broken items).
  Note: capped at the year's rental income; excess carries forward 4 years.
- **Improvements** are NOT deductible expenses — they add to acquisition
  cost and amortise at 3% along with the building value.
- **Agency fees, legal fees**, tenant-screening, utilities if you pay them.
- **Local services tax** (basuras, alcantarillado).

### 1.3 Imputed income on unused secondary residences

- Empty secondary homes (not primary, not rented) have **imputed income**:
  - **1.1%** of catastral value if the municipality revalued in the last
    10 years.
  - **2%** otherwise.
- Enters base general. No expenses deductible against imputed income.
- Primary residence is **exempt** from imputation.

## 2. Local recurring taxes

### 2.1 IBI (Impuesto sobre Bienes Inmuebles)

- Annual municipal tax on the catastral value.
- Typical rate **0.4%–1.1%** of catastral value (municipality sets).
- Canary Islands IBI rates are at the lower end (most municipalities
  0.45%–0.6%).
- Due on the owner as of January 1.
- **Deductible only when the property is rented** (pro-rated for partial-
  year rental). Primary-residence and unused secondary IBI is not
  deductible for individuals.

### 2.2 Basuras / alcantarillado / vado

- Municipal services (garbage, sewage, curb-cut licences).
- Deductible only when the property is rented.

### 2.3 Tasa de incremento de valor de los terrenos (plusvalía municipal)

- Municipal capital-gains-like tax on **sale of urban land**.
- Paid by the seller within 30 days of sale (gift: by the donee; inherited
  property: by the heir, 6-month window).
- Since the 2021 constitutional ruling, the filer can choose the method:
  1. **Objective** — legal formula on catastral value × years held.
  2. **Real** — difference between sale and acquisition value, taxed at
     the municipal rate.
- If there was **no real gain** (sale ≤ acquisition), plusvalía **cannot
  be levied**. Keep acquisition invoices forever.

## 3. Transactional taxes (on purchase)

### 3.1 Second-hand property

- **ITP (Impuesto sobre Transmisiones Patrimoniales)** — regional tax
  on buying used property.
- **Canarias general rate**: **6.5%** for residential (vs. state default
  of 7–10% in other CCAA).
- Reduced rates for primary residence of young buyers, large families,
  disabled, and rural repopulation in some islands (4%).
- Paid by the buyer within 30 days of notarial deed.

### 3.2 New-build property (from developer)

- **IGIC at 7%** (Canary Islands) instead of the 10% VAT other CCAA pay.
  Bonificaciones exist for first-time buyer primary residence.
- **AJD (Actos Jurídicos Documentados)** on the deed — Canarias rate:
  **1%** of the scriptura value (other CCAA: 0.5%–2%).

## 4. Capital gain on property sale (Modelo 100)

Sales fall under **ganancias patrimoniales** → base del ahorro (19–28%).

- **Gain** = (Sale price − sale costs) − (Acquisition price + acquisition
  costs + improvements + amortizations that reduced prior-year rental
  rendimiento).
- Amortisations taken during rental years **reduce** acquisition cost,
  increasing the realised gain when sold. This is often missed.
- Negative gains offset other ahorro gains; excess losses offset up to
  25% of capital mobiliario income; remainder carries 4 years.

### 4.1 Primary-residence exemptions

- **Full exemption on reinvestment**: if proceeds are reinvested in a new
  primary residence within **2 years** (or up to 2 years before sale),
  the gain is exempt proportionally to the reinvested amount.
- **Age 65+ exemption**: seniors selling their primary residence owe no
  IRPF on the gain, regardless of reinvestment.
- **Age 65+ reinvestment into an annuity** (rentas vitalicias): up to
  **€240,000** exempt when reinvested into a qualifying annuity within
  6 months.

### 4.2 Tourist rentals owned through individual

- Gain on sale is still ahorro; rental years already taxed normally.
- If hotel-like services were provided and activity treatment was applied,
  the property may be a business asset — gain may have a different
  character (consult a gestor; Taxinator treats this as edge-case).

## 5. Wealth tax (Modelo 714)

- Annual filing if total net worth exceeds the regional threshold.
- **Canarias baseline threshold: €700,000** (primary residence excluded
  up to €300,000).
- **Canarias applies a 50% bonificación** to the final cuota — net rate
  is half of the scale (~0.1%–1.75%).
- Property values used: greater of (a) acquisition value, (b) catastral
  value, (c) value assigned by AEAT on inspection.
- Mortgages **reduce** the property's net value for Modelo 714.
- Impuesto de Solidaridad de Grandes Fortunas (state-level, net worth
  > €3M) layers on top for very wealthy filers — Canarias bonificación
  doesn't shield from it.

## 6. Filing calendar

| Event | Form | Deadline |
|-------|------|----------|
| Annual Modelo 100 (rental rendimiento + any sale gain) | Modelo 100 | End of June |
| Wealth tax | Modelo 714 | Same window as Modelo 100 |
| Purchase of used property | ITP (autonómico) | 30 days from deed |
| Purchase of new-build | AJD | 30 days from deed |
| Municipal plusvalía on sale | municipal form | 30 days from deed |
| IBI annual payment | municipal | Varies by municipality (typically summer) |

## 7. What Taxinator tracks for property

- **`income_sources` with kind='rental'** — one row per property.
  - `metadata.address`, `metadata.rentalType` (`long` | `short` | `commercial`),
    `metadata.ownershipPct`, `metadata.hasMortgage`,
    `metadata.constructionYear`, `metadata.catastralValue`,
    `metadata.constructionValueCents`.
- **Rental-income transactions** with `status='personal_income'`,
  `income_source_id` set, `type='income'`.
- **Rental-expense transactions** in rental-specific categories
  (`rental_maintenance`, `rental_insurance`, `rental_ibi`,
  `rental_community_fees`, `rental_amortization`, `rental_interest`) with
  `income_source_id` set to link them to the property.
- **Personal deductions** for IBI on primary residence — not deductible for
  IRPF but tracked for completeness and wealth-tax calculation.
- Modelo 100 then computes `rendimientosCapitalInmobiliario` = sum of net
  per-property figures, applying the 60%/70%/90% reduction only to
  long-term residential.

## 8. Red flags

- **Undeclared rental**: if AEAT cross-references with census / utility
  records and finds undeclared tenants, the 60% reduction is lost
  retroactively and penalties layer on top.
- **Mixing primary residence deduction with imputed income**: a property
  can be primary residence OR secondary-for-imputation for the same year,
  not both. Prorate by days if you moved.
- **Forgetting amortisation in sale cost basis**: you must reduce
  acquisition price by the amortisation claimed during rental years.
- **Short-term rentals with services** that look like activity — getting
  this wrong shifts the income from base general to a completely
  different regime (autónomo with IGIC obligations).
- **Foreign property** owners who are Spanish tax residents must declare
  worldwide rental income AND report the asset on **Modelo 720** if value
  > €50,000, plus potential Modelo 721 if crypto-backed.
