# Spanish personal IRPF — Modelo 100 (2026 rules)

> Last verified: 2026-04-01. Applies to individual residents filing annual IRPF.
> Regional rates reflect Canary Islands defaults where relevant; other CCAA
> differ slightly on base-general bumps and family-deduction ceilings.

## 1. Filing basics

- **Modelo 100** is the annual personal income tax return ("Declaración de la
  Renta"). Campaign opens early **April** and closes **end of June**.
- Tax year is the calendar year (Jan 1 – Dec 31). You file the year after.
- **Residents** (183+ days in Spain, or centre of economic interest here)
  declare **worldwide income**. Non-residents use Modelo 210 instead.
- The AEAT offers a pre-filled draft ("borrador") in early April — always
  review it; errors on rental, crypto, and foreign income are common.

## 2. Income categories (rendimientos)

Modelo 100 sorts income into five categories that feed two separate bases:

| Category | Spanish label | Feeds |
|----------|---------------|-------|
| Employment | rendimientos del trabajo | base general |
| Real estate capital | rendimientos del capital inmobiliario | base general |
| Economic activity | rendimientos de actividades económicas | base general |
| Movable capital | rendimientos del capital mobiliario | base del ahorro |
| Capital gains / losses | ganancias y pérdidas patrimoniales | base del ahorro (if from transfer) |

### 2.1 Employment (rendimientos del trabajo)

- Gross salary, pensions, severance, certain in-kind benefits (company car,
  housing, stock options).
- Deductible from gross:
  - **Employee SS contribution** (full amount withheld).
  - **€2,000 standard gastos deducibles** — no receipts needed (2026 rule).
  - **€7,500** for recognised disabled workers (33–65% disability).
  - **Union dues** and **mandatory professional colegio fees** (capped €500).
- **Reducción por rendimientos del trabajo** for low income: starts at
  **€5,565** and phases out between €14,047.50 and €19,747.50 of net work
  income, fully gone above.
- Cross-reference: see the **Crypto tax** pack when salary is partly paid in
  crypto or RSUs are involved.

### 2.2 Real estate capital (rendimientos del capital inmobiliario)

Full coverage lives in the **Property tax** pack. Summary for Modelo 100:

- Long-term residential rental: **60% reduction** on net positive rendimiento.
- Tourist / short-term / commercial rental: no reduction; may fall under
  activity if services are provided (cleaning, reception, breakfasts).
- Imputed income for empty secondary residences: **1.1%** of catastral value
  if revalued in last 10 years, else **2%**.

### 2.3 Economic activity (rendimientos de actividades económicas)

See the **Canary autónomo** pack. Net activity income (ingresos − gastos
deducibles − amortizaciones) goes into base general of Modelo 100. For
autónomos who are **also** the Modelo 100 filer, Taxinator folds the business
rendimiento and the personal streams into a single unified calculation.

### 2.4 Movable capital (rendimientos del capital mobiliario)

- Bank interest (already gross-withheld 19%).
- Dividends from Spanish and foreign shares (first €1,500 exemption was
  repealed in 2015; today **every euro is taxable**).
- Insurance-contract payouts and certain annuities.
- **Staking rewards and crypto lending interest** are classified here —
  taxed at base del ahorro rates, not ordinary income. See **Crypto tax**.

### 2.5 Capital gains / losses (ganancias y pérdidas patrimoniales)

- **Transfer-based** (from selling an asset) → base del ahorro.
  - Stock / ETF / fund disposals — mandatory **FIFO** cost basis per ISIN.
  - Crypto disposals — mandatory FIFO per asset (BTC, ETH, …).
  - Property sale gains — shorter-term sales and primary-residence exemption
    (reinvestment, ≥65) covered in the Property tax pack.
- **Non-transfer gains** (lotteries, prizes, subsidies) → base general.
- **Loss offsetting**: losses in ahorro category offset ahorro gains; any
  excess offsets up to **25%** of ahorro income from capital mobiliario
  (interest/dividends). Remaining losses carry forward **4 years**.

## 3. Base liquidable

After summing rendimientos into each base and applying reducible deductions:

- **Base liquidable general** = base general − reducible deductions (pension
  contributions, compensatory alimony, etc.).
- **Base liquidable del ahorro** = base ahorro − any reducible deductions
  still applicable to ahorro.

## 4. 2026 tax brackets

### 4.1 Base general (state + Canarias autonómica combined, approximate)

Brackets below are the **combined** rate (state + regional). Other CCAA vary.

| From | To | Combined rate |
|------|----|---------------|
| 0 | 12,450 | **19%** |
| 12,450 | 20,200 | **24%** |
| 20,200 | 35,200 | **30%** |
| 35,200 | 60,000 | **37%** |
| 60,000 | 300,000 | **45%** |
| 300,000+ | — | **47%** |

### 4.2 Base del ahorro (applies to dividends, interest, gains)

| From | To | Rate |
|------|----|------|
| 0 | 6,000 | **19%** |
| 6,000 | 50,000 | **21%** |
| 50,000 | 200,000 | **23%** |
| 200,000 | 300,000 | **27%** |
| 300,000+ | — | **28%** |

## 5. Deductions on the base (reducen base imponible)

Applied **before** the brackets are run, so they effectively save at the
filer's marginal rate.

- **Pension plan contributions**: personal cap **€1,500/year**. Employer
  contributions add up to €8,500 more (joint cap €10,000).
- **Plan de Pensiones de Empleo** (collective pension): additional
  contribution room up to €8,500 joint with employer.
- **Compensatory alimony to ex-spouse** (judicial): fully deductible from
  base general.
- **Aportaciones a sistemas de previsión social del cónyuge**: spouse with
  income < €8,000 → up to €1,000/year.

## 6. Deductions on the cuota (reducen cuota íntegra)

Applied **after** brackets. Value is independent of marginal rate.

- **Donations to registered ONG (Ley 49/2002)**:
  - **80%** on the first **€250**.
  - **40%** on the amount above €250.
  - **45%** if the same beneficiary received donations from the filer in the
    two previous years at or above the prior-year amount (recurring bonus).
- **Mortgage on primary residence**: **only pre-2013** acquisition still
  qualifies — 15% of payments up to **€9,040** base (max deduction €1,356).
- **Family**: per dependent child, per dependent elder, disability
  multipliers. Per-child deduction rises for subsequent children
  (€2,400 / €2,700 / €4,000 / €4,500).
- **Canarias regional deductions**:
  - **Rent on primary residence** (taxable income < €20,000): up to 20% of
    rent paid, capped at €600 (or €840 for ≥65 or disabled).
  - **Childcare expenses** (children under 3): €400/year.
  - **Healthcare expenses**: 10% of out-of-pocket medical fees.
  - **Education**: €100/child for school supplies.
  - **Transporte colectivo** (public transport): 10% capped €100.

## 7. Retenciones (withholdings)

Every retention is a **credit** against the final cuota:

- **Employment** — employer withholds progressive IRPF from each payroll.
- **Freelance invoicing** — autónomo invoices to Spanish businesses typically
  include a 15% IRPF retention (7% for the first two years of activity).
- **Dividends / interest** — paying entity withholds **19%** at source.
- **Rental income** — tenant businesses withhold 19% when renting from
  individuals (residential rentals to individuals: no retention).

Total retenciones − cuota líquida = **cuota diferencial**. Positive =
refund due; negative = extra payment owed.

## 8. Filing triggers (non-exhaustive)

You **must** file if any of these apply:

- Employment income **> €22,000** from a single payer.
- Employment income **> €15,876** from multiple payers when the 2nd+ payer
  aggregated paid more than €1,500.
- **Rental** income > €1,000 gross.
- **Capital gains** > €1,600.
- Any autónomo activity at all.
- Pension / annuity receipts in certain combinations.
- **Modelo 721** informativa requirement on foreign crypto > €50K.

When in doubt, file — the pre-filled borrador is usually fine and filing with
no additional tax due is free.

## 9. What Taxinator tracks for Modelo 100

- **`income_sources`** rows (kind: `salary` / `rental` / `dividend` /
  `interest` / `other`) — reusable per-employer/landlord/broker records.
- **`transactions` with `status='personal_income'`** and
  `income_source_id` set — the actual cash inflows.
- **Crypto + stock disposals** via the shared FIFO ledger
  (`crypto_lots`, `crypto_disposal_matches` with `asset_class` dimension).
- **`personal_deductions`** rows (kind: `pension` / `mortgage` / `donation`
  / `family` / `regional` / `other`) with supporting files.
- **Business activity rendimiento** from invoices and deductible expenses —
  only on autónomo entity profiles, folded into the same unified Modelo 100.

## 10. Red flags

- **Residency** — centre of economic interest can pull non-obvious cases
  into Spanish tax (digital nomads, split-year moves).
- **Foreign income double-counting** — always check the double-tax treaty
  and apply the deducción por doble imposición internacional on Modelo 100
  casilla 588+.
- **Reconciling employer retention** — if nómina IRPF withheld doesn't
  match the bracket math, the employer's estimate used a simplified formula.
  File normally; cuota diferencial corrects it.
- **Crypto borrador omissions** — AEAT's pre-filled draft rarely includes
  crypto disposals. Never sign the draft without reviewing the Crypto tax
  pack's realisation rules.
