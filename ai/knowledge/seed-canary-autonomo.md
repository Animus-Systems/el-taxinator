# Canary Islands — Autónomo tax knowledge pack

Last verified: 2026-04-15 · Source: Taxinator seed
Applies to: self-employed (autónomo) residents whose tax domicile is in the
Canary Islands under the IGIC regime (Islas Canarias, REF).

## IGIC — Impuesto General Indirecto Canario

IGIC replaces IVA (VAT) in the Canary Islands. Administered by the Agencia
Tributaria Canaria (ATC). Standard bands (verify against the most recent
Ley 20/1991 update before filing):

| Rate | Name (ES) | Typical applicability |
|------|-----------|------------------------|
| 0%   | Tipo cero | Basic groceries, books, medicines |
| 3%   | Tipo reducido | Certain food, water, specific services |
| 7%   | Tipo general | Default rate for most goods/services |
| 9.5% | Tipo incrementado | Specific luxury/alcoholic products |
| 15%  | Tipo especial | Tobacco and selected goods |
| 20%  | Tipo especial incrementado | Specific high-value items |

Reference: Ley 20/1991 (REF canario), updated by annual Ley de Presupuestos.

## Quarterly filings — autónomo calendar

| Form | Covers | Filing window |
|------|--------|---------------|
| Modelo 420 | IGIC self-assessment — supported & charged | Month after quarter end (Apr 20, Jul 20, Oct 20, Jan 30) |
| Modelo 130 | IRPF quarterly fractional payment (20% net income) | Same windows as 420 |
| Modelo 425 | Annual IGIC summary | Jan 30 (covering Q4 + year recap) |
| Modelo 100 | Annual IRPF | Apr–Jun (following year) |
| Modelo 303 | Only for non-Canary activities (IVA) | Same windows as 420 |

Quarterly deadlines:
- Q1 (Jan–Mar) → April 20
- Q2 (Apr–Jun) → July 20
- Q3 (Jul–Sep) → October 20
- Q4 (Oct–Dec) → January 30 of the following year (Modelo 420 + 425)

## Income classification

- **Sales / services** to clients in the Canary Islands: IGIC repercutido at
  the applicable rate (usually 7%). Mark on Modelo 420 casilla 1/2 (base)
  and casilla 3/4 (cuota).
- **Professional services** to Spanish (mainland) business clients: include
  IRPF retention at 15% (7% for new autónomos in the first two years under
  Art. 101.5 LIRPF). Issue factura with retained amount deducted — the
  client pays the retention on your behalf via Modelo 111.
- **EU B2B sales** (reverse charge): 0% IGIC on the invoice, declare under
  Modelo 349 if in excess of the intrastat threshold.
- **Exports outside EU**: 0% IGIC, note the IGIC-exempt clause on the factura.

## Deductibility rules — what autónomos can deduct

Expenses are deductible on Modelo 130 (and annual Modelo 100) only when they
are **afectos a la actividad** (directly linked to the business activity),
have a valid **factura** with the autónomo's NIF, and are properly recorded.

### 100% deductible (with valid factura)

- Office supplies, software subscriptions (e.g. design tools, hosting).
- Professional services: accountants, lawyers, consultants.
- Rent of dedicated business premises.
- Insurance premiums for the business activity (e.g. professional liability).
- Social Security autónomo quotas (cuota de autónomos) — per Art. 30.2.1
  LIRPF.
- Bank fees on accounts used exclusively for the business.
- Utilities and internet for a dedicated business premises.

### 50% deductible (mixed-use rule)

- Restaurant meals with clients (comida de trabajo): 50% of the cost,
  capped at €26.67/day in Spain or €48.08/day abroad per Art. 30.2 LIRPF,
  and only when there is a factura with NIF and a documented business
  purpose (guests, agenda).
- Business vehicle expenses when the vehicle is used for both personal and
  business: 50% of fuel, maintenance, insurance, and depreciation. 100% is
  only allowed for taxis, driving-school vehicles, or when the vehicle is
  demonstrably exclusive-use (Art. 22.4 RIS).

### Home-office (uso parcial de vivienda)

- If a defined percentage of the home (e.g. 20%) is declared as office space,
  the autónomo can deduct that percentage of:
  - Mortgage interest / rent
  - IBI, basura, community fees
  - Home utilities (electricity, water, gas): only 30% of the declared
    percentage is deductible for utilities (Art. 30.2.5 LIRPF).
- Requires a communication to the tax office (Modelo 036/037) declaring
  the percentage.

### Not deductible

- Personal groceries.
- Family meals and entertainment without a clear business context.
- Clothing (unless uniforms with business logo).
- Private vehicle expenses when business use cannot be demonstrated.
- Fines, traffic tickets, late-filing surcharges.
- Gifts to clients exceeding 1% of annual turnover (Art. 15 LIS-analogous).

## Common tax-saving tips (cite the rule)

- Convert a personal meal into a deductible one by inviting a client and
  requesting a proper factura con NIF (50% deductible, Art. 30.2.1 LIRPF).
- Keep a driver's log when using your car for business calls — the log plus
  invoices justifies the 50% mixed-use deduction.
- Buy hardware / software that clearly supports the declared activity code
  (IAE / CNAE). Productivity tools, project management, design software
  are typically 100% deductible with a valid factura.
- Prepay annual software subscriptions before quarter-end to accelerate
  deduction into the current Modelo 130.
- File Modelo 036 promptly when starting a home-office or mixed-use
  account arrangement — retroactive deductions are disallowed.

## Cryptocurrency

Canary Islands tax treatment of crypto matches mainland Spain — IGIC does not
apply to crypto disposals (intangible financial assets). Relevant rules:

- **Disposals = ganancia/pérdida patrimonial.** Sale, exchange, or withdrawal
  of crypto for fiat or another asset produces a capital gain/loss, taxed on
  the **base del ahorro** in Modelo 100 at progressive rates.
- **2026 base del ahorro brackets (subject to change — verify):**
  - 19% on first €6,000
  - 21% on €6,000–€50,000
  - 23% on €50,000–€200,000
  - 27% on €200,000–€300,000
  - 28% above €300,000
- **FIFO is mandatory** for cost basis. "First in, first out" across all
  holdings of the same asset. Legal basis: Art. 14.1.c LIRPF, Art. 37 LIRPF.
- **Staking / lending / yield = rendimiento del capital mobiliario.** Taxed
  on the base del ahorro at the same rate schedule above but reported on
  Modelo 100 under a different casilla. Recognize at fair market value at
  the moment of receipt.
- **Airdrops and hard forks** = ganancia patrimonial with zero acquisition
  value; taxable at FMV on receipt.
- **Loss offsetting.** Net disposal losses can offset disposal gains fully,
  then up to 25% of rendimiento del capital mobiliario; remainder carries
  forward 4 years (Art. 49 LIRPF).
- **Modelo 721 (declaración informativa).** If the aggregate value held on
  foreign exchanges/wallets exceeds €50,000 at 31 December, or increased by
  more than €20,000 vs last year's declaration, the taxpayer must file
  Modelo 721 between 1 January and 31 March the following year. This is
  informational — no tax due — but non-filing penalties are material.
- **No IGIC on crypto trades.** Trading crypto → crypto or crypto → EUR on
  an exchange is not an IGIC-taxable supply, so disposals do not appear on
  Modelo 420.

When a user reports a Swissborg/Coinbase/Binance/Kraken bank deposit, the
tax event is the disposal that produced that fiat — NOT the bank credit.
Pair the bank row with its disposal via the gateway-link feature so the
bank deposit is not double-counted as professional income.

## Red flags (reject or reclassify)

- Receipt without NIF → not a valid factura, not deductible.
- Round-number cash withdrawals coded to "office supplies".
- Personal supermarket shops coded as "meals with clients".
- Utility bills at the home address with no Modelo 036 declaration.
- Car expenses for a private vehicle with no mileage log.
- Crypto exchange withdrawal coded as "other_income" — must be
  crypto_disposal with a matching cost basis.
